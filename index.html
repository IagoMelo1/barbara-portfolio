Refreshing the Guinness brand for Gen Z

𐂂 Guinness
← Back to Work

Client Challenge

Guinness needs to modernize its brand appeal to better resonate with Gen Z drinkers while preserving its core brand values of quality and craft.

Context

  • An established and respected brand, but seen as traditional and old-fashioned.
  • Associated mainly with older drinkers in pubs.

Insight

Client Challenge
Guinness is perceived as traditional and associated with older generations.
Insight
Younger drinkers appreciate legacy brands but want them presented in new relevant ways.
Opportunity
Tap into the authenticity of Guinness and present it through a modern, digital-first lens.

Proposed Direction

  • Keep the iconic Guinness heritage but showcase it in fresh, contemporary contexts.
  • Develop digital content that highlights unique Guinness rituals.
  • Engage with Gen Z through interactive and experiential activations.

Improving brand perception among young travelers

Client Challenge

Ryanair needs to improve brand perception among younger travelers while maintaining its low-cost positioning.

Context

  • Ryanair is well known as a low-cost airline focused on affordable travel.
  • Their communication is often perceived as blunt and unpolished.

Insight

Client Challenge
Ryanair struggles with a negative perception among young travelers.
Insight
Younger audiences are drawn to authentic, unpolished brands that don’t try to be perfect.
Opportunity
Lean into Ryanair’s current tone of voice and make it a core part of their brand identity.

Proposed Direction

  • Embrace Ryanair’s irreverent tone of voice.
  • Develop a social media strategy that uses humor and relatability as key elements.
  • Expand reactive marketing to join online trends that resonate with Gen Z.
